The firm

Independent. Operator-led. Texas-based.

Westgate Financial Services is an independent bookkeeping and QuickBooks ProAdvisor practice. We are not a CPA firm, and we are not a software company. We do one thing: operational bookkeeping, performed by senior operators who stay on your file from the first month through every month after.

The firm handles cleanup, catch-up, monthly bookkeeping, bank and account reconciliations, and operational accounting for Texas small businesses, nonprofits, and churches — run inside QuickBooks Online and Desktop by the same team. If you engage WestgateFS, the operators who begin your file are the operators who run it. No rotation, no hand-offs, no context loss.

We work alongside CPAs, not in competition with them. Our scope is the operational bookkeeping that makes their work possible — clean, reconciled records delivered every period. We do not file tax returns, provide tax advice, or perform audit services. When clients need a CPA referral, we help make that introduction.

“Clean books aren’t a software outcome. They’re the result of someone paying attention — on purpose, every month.”

The founder

Built by an operator, for operators.

David Westgate founded the practice after four decades in operational bookkeeping across Texas industries — restaurants, construction, professional services, nonprofits, and churches. He built WestgateFS around a specific conviction: that real bookkeeping quality comes from experienced operators paying consistent attention, not from software automation and thin staffing.

As a QuickBooks ProAdvisor, David manages bookkeeping inside QuickBooks Online and Desktop for clients across Texas. Every engagement reflects the same standard: every account reconciled to source, every period documented, CPA-ready books delivered without drama. The same operators who start an engagement are the ones who run it — month after month, without rotation.

David is frank about scope. WestgateFS does not provide tax advice, tax filing, audit services, or Intuit technical support. When clients need a CPA or a specialist, the firm refers them honestly — and helps make that introduction. The firm was built to do one thing well, not to be everything to everyone.

“The work is in the judgment — knowing what the number means, whether it’s right, and what to do when it isn’t. That judgment comes from experience, not software.”

How we operate

Four things every WestgateFS engagement holds to.

Not promises. Not marketing language. The operating standards the firm was built around and has maintained since the beginning.

01

Same operators, every month.

The operator who starts your file is the one who runs it. No rotation, no re-onboarding, no lost context when something changes in your business. Continuity is structural — not a commitment made and quietly broken.

02

Reconciled to source. Always.

Every account, every period. Not reconciled to what we think happened — reconciled to the actual bank statement, credit card statement, and source document. Variances investigated and resolved, not papered over with a journal entry.

03

CPA-ready books, every period.

Your books are maintained to the standard your CPA needs every month — not assembled under pressure at year-end. Tax season is a clean handoff, not a rescue. Your CPA should receive organized records and get straight to filing.

04

Honest about fit — including when we’re not.

If your situation is better served by a CPA, a specialist, or a different type of practice, we say so — directly, at the first conversation. We’d rather refer you correctly than take an engagement that isn’t right for either side.
