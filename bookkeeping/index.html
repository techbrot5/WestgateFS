Bookkeeping cleanup, catch-up bookkeeping, monthly bookkeeping, account reconciliations, outsourced bookkeeping, and QuickBooks ProAdvisor management of QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop — for small businesses, nonprofits, and churches across Texas.

Bookkeeping FAQ

Direct answers to the bookkeeping questions Texas owners ask.

What is bookkeeping and what does WestgateFS handle?

Bookkeeping is the systematic recording, categorizing, and reconciliation of every financial transaction your business makes. WestgateFS handles bookkeeping cleanup, catch-up bookkeeping, monthly bookkeeping, reconciliations, outsourced bookkeeping, and QuickBooks ProAdvisor management of QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop — for small businesses, nonprofits, and churches across Texas. We are not a CPA firm and deliver CPA-ready books to your CPA.

What’s the difference between bookkeeping cleanup and catch-up bookkeeping?

Bookkeeping cleanup fixes books that exist but are wrong — miscategorized transactions, unreconciled accounts, broken reports. Catch-up bookkeeping addresses books that don’t yet exist for a period — months or years of unrecorded activity. Many engagements involve both: catch-up the missing periods, then clean up what was previously recorded.

How is monthly bookkeeping priced?

Monthly bookkeeping is priced based on transaction volume, the number of bank and credit card accounts, the complexity of your industry, and the reporting cadence you need. Most Texas small businesses fall into honest, predictable monthly ranges. We never quote until we’ve reviewed a recent month of activity. See our pricing approach.

How long does bookkeeping cleanup take?

Most bookkeeping cleanups take between four and ten weeks, depending on how many months are out of order, how many accounts need reconciliation, and how clean the source documents are. We give a written timeline after a free diagnostic review of your QuickBooks file. More on bookkeeping cleanup.

Do you only work in QuickBooks?

Primarily, yes. We are a QuickBooks ProAdvisor practice and we run our bookkeeping inside QuickBooks Online or QuickBooks Desktop — the platforms most Texas small businesses already use. If you’re on a different system, we’ll discuss whether migration to QuickBooks makes sense before any engagement begins. Explore QuickBooks management.

Is outsourced bookkeeping right for my small business?

Outsourced bookkeeping makes sense when you don’t need a full-time in-house bookkeeper but still need a reconciled monthly close, reconciled accounts, and CPA-ready reports. Most of our outsourced bookkeeping clients are Texas small businesses in the $250K–$15M range. More on outsourced bookkeeping.

Will you coordinate with my CPA?

Yes — coordinating with your CPA is part of the work. We deliver clean monthly bookkeeping, year-end CPA-ready books, and any specific schedules your CPA requests. We do not prepare tax returns, audits, or other CPA-regulated work ourselves; we make your CPA’s job faster.

Is my financial data secure?

Yes. We use encrypted file sharing, least-privilege access controls, multi-factor authentication on every system, and secure QuickBooks accountant access — never shared admin logins. Read our data security practices.
